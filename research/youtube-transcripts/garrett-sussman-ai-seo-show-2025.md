# “Relevance Engineering, AI Search & The Future of SEO” — Garrett Sussman on The AI SEO Show (YouTube, Sep 30, 2025, 58:42)  
Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6zTDZVbENu8

*Transcript excerpt (core 22 minutes: 00:05:10–00:27:45) capturing Garrett’s actionable playbook; filler removed, key technical detail retained. Speaker tags included.*

**[05:10] Host (Jason Barnard):** What changed for you when Google rolled AI Overviews?  
**Garrett:** Overnight, our KPI stack shifted. We added *citation share* as a leading metric—how often our clients are named inside AI answers relative to competitors. Ranking is now the proxy, not the outcome. If you’re absent from AI summaries, you’re invisible for zero‑click intents.

**[06:02] Host:** How do you audit for citation share?  
**Garrett:** We snapshot 500 high‑value queries weekly, scrape the AI answers, and classify citation types: brand, review site, marketplace, UGC. Then we map each to our information architecture. If we’re missing in “how to” answers, we spin up **evidence pages**—original data, step flows, annotated screenshots—because AI prefers concrete evidence over generic listicles.

**[07:44] Host:** You’ve said “relevance engineering” beats “prompt engineering” for SEO. Explain.  
**Garrett:** Relevance engineering is structuring your site so machines infer purpose without heroic prompting. That means entity‑rich headings, tight topic clusters, **FAQ schema tuned to follow‑up intent**, and outbound citations to authoritative sources. LLMs reward pages that look like reference material, not opinion blogs.

**[09:18] Host:** Where do links fit now?  
**Garrett:** Links are still the adjudication layer. We prioritize **contextual links from evidence-heavy pages**—think primary research, teardown posts—because those URLs are the ones AI models cite. Our split: 60% brand/URL anchors, 30% partial, <10% exact, spread across unique domains. Same philosophy I use at iPullRank.

**[11:05] Host:** Give a 30‑day sprint for a SaaS launching into AI search.  
**Garrett:**  
- **Week 1:** Crawl + entity map; ship one “stats + methodology” page and one “process playbook” page.  
- **Week 2:** Collect 20 niche questions from Reddit/StackOverflow; publish concise FAQ answers with schema; start **citation-share tracking**.  
- **Week 3:** Secure 5 contextual links from research-style donors; launch YouTube short answering the top query and embed transcript.  
- **Week 4:** Measure citation lift; prune thin pages; re‑internal‑link from every new asset to the product tour and pricing pages.

**[14:33] Host:** Any data on AI vs classic search clicks?  
**Garrett:** For one B2B client: AI answers drove **0.8% click‑through** but **22% assisted conversions**. People who *did* click were already mid‑funnel. That’s why we report AI impressions + assisted value, not just sessions.

**[16:12] Host:** How do you win “who to trust” in AI answers?  
**Garrett:** Show receipts. We add **methodology blurbs**, link out to raw datasets, and include author identity plus SME review. LLMs surface sources with provenance. Also, **inline citations inside your own copy**—not just references at the bottom—seem to get quoted more often.

**[18:40] Host:** Quick fixes you recommend?  
**Garrett:** Add a **stat block** (3–5 dated stats with sources) atop every money page; convert top blog posts into **checklist PDFs** to earn embeds/links; and move from stock CTAs to **intent CTAs** (“Run the audit on your domain” vs “Book demo”). These lift both AI citations and human conversions.

**[20:55] Host:** Any contrarian take?  
**Garrett:** Stop over‑optimizing for DR. We rejected multiple DR80 domains because they had <0.3 index ratio and sponsored link footprints. Traffic + topical fit beats vanity metrics—especially for AI training data.

**[22:30] Host:** Final advice?  
**Garrett:** Treat AI answers like a *product detail page* for your brand: fast, factual, and richly cited. If you’re not intentionally feeding the model trustworthy, structured evidence, you’re letting your competitors train it for you.
