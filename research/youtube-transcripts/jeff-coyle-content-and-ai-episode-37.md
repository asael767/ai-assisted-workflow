Transcript (excerpt from published transcript on episode page):

Host Ryan Goldman: “Generative AI has changed the way we think about content creation. It's now a reference tool for pasting and repurposing everything we’ve already learned.”

Jeff Coyle: “It’s been that way forever. Technology makes us better because we can remember everything we've captured — everything we've learned. When you get used to having all the knowledge you’ve captured, it’s inevitable you’re going to want something that allows you to pull it all together, whether it's from a source of documents or a source of earlier chats that you've had. It's not what people keep saying they use it for, which is typically what’s called wrap-up. Those are the folks that are like, ‘I have this meeting. We talked forever. Give me the executive summary.’ But there are so many different avenues where keeping that knowledge is helpful.”

Coyle continues: “I use it for a variety of things. I'm going to be using it more for memory retrieval, and that eliminates a ‘why not’ scenario. It might not replace lots of things. It will not replace editors. It will not replace subject matter experts, but it will make them much better.”
