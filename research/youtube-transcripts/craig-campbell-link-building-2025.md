**[08:30] Craig Campbell:** Most link building still fails because people buy volume, not outcomes. I set a ceiling of 10 new referring domains per week for a fresh site—anything faster looks like manipulation. Anchor mix: ~65% brand/URL, 25% topical partials, <10% exact, each on unique donors.

**[10:05] Gerrid Smith:** How do you pick donors?  
**Craig:** DR is the last column. I start with *index ratio* (indexed URLs ÷ total URLs) and *organic traffic* in the target language. If a site has <1k organic visits/month or >15% of recent posts tagged sponsored/ugc, it’s off the list. I also check server‑side rendering; JS-only sites are link equity dead-ends for AI systems.

**[12:40] Craig:** Outreach that still works in 2025: personalized Looms pointing out a broken stat or outdated year; offer a replacement stat block (with sources) and a co-branded graphic. Stop at message three—domain reputation matters more than a single placement.

**[15:10] Craig:** AI Overviews changed measurement. We track *citation share* inside AI answers for 300 queries. If we’re retrieved but not cited, we add inline citations and methodology blurbs to the page. Citations without clicks still drive *assisted conversions* through branded searches.

**[17:55] Craig:** Press releases? Only when paired with internal link rewiring the same day. Otherwise equity dies on an island.  

**[19:20] Craig:** Quick-win internal linking sprint: take your top 20 traffic pages and add 2–3 contextual links each to the new asset with partial-match anchors. It’s the cheapest authority transfer you’ll ever do.

**[21:05] Craig:** For AI crawlability: expose a JSON “source-of-truth” file with brand claims, pricing rules, and policies. Link it in robots.txt and sitemaps. It reduces hallucinations in AI answers and keeps product data consistent across AI surfaces.

**[23:40] Gerrid:** Any mistakes to avoid?  
**Craig:** Buying expired domains without checking historical anchors; stacking exact-match anchors on the same donor; ignoring log files. Log files tell you if Bingbot/GPTBot actually render your JS. If they don’t, fix rendering before buying links.

**[25:55] Craig:** 30-day playbook for a new SaaS:
- Week 1: crawl + entity map; publish one stats page and one use-case page.  
- Week 2: secure 5 contextual links from research-style donors; start internal link sprint.  
- Week 3: run A/B redirect test on two mid-tier donors to verify quality before pointing to money pages.  
- Week 4: prune risky anchors; add FAQ/HowTo schema; measure citation share lift and assisted conversions.
