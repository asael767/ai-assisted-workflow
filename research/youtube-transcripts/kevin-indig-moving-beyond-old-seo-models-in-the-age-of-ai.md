# Moving Beyond Old SEO Models in the Age of AI — Kevin Indig (YouTube, 2026-01-19)
Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YGveIxNTyLC

Transcript excerpt (key minutes)
- Host (Gianluca Fiorelli): AI is making search more natural to the user by enabling conversational and image search. How does that change content creation?
- Kevin Indig: Historically we built content around explicit queries. Now we have to watch how people prompt and infer the intent behind the prompt. Content must map to intents and entities the model can stitch together.

- Host: How should marketers adapt measurement?
- Kevin: Move from ranking to influence. I’m looking at citation share inside AI answers as a primary KPI. Rankings and traffic are decoupled from pipeline; influence and recall matter more.

- Host: What’s the role of proprietary data?
- Kevin: Proprietary data is the moat. Use the data only your users generate and publish insights around it—that’s what LLMs trust and cite.

- Host: Is it time to unlearn keyword thinking?
- Kevin: Yes. We need to unlearn the tight connection between keywords and content. Optimize to be the most credible source the AI selects, not just to rank for a term.

- Host: Can small teams keep up?
- Kevin: AI-assisted workflows let small teams publish 10x faster without sacrificing quality. Execution speed and clear information architecture become the differentiator.
