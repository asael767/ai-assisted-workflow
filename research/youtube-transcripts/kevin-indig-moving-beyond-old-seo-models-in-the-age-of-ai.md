Host (Gianluca Fiorelli): AI is making search more natural to the user by enabling conversational and image search.

Kevin: For sure. Historically, we built content by seeing queries in search engines and crafting content around the query. Now it feels more like watching someone generate a prompt and infer what their intent behind the prompt is and how we can help them with our content.

Host: And how should marketers adapt?

Kevin: How do you drive people back to your site, how do you go from the answer that is generated in an AI, an agent, a chat, to the actual conversion? What is it that you offer that is so different that they’re convinced they should go from an AI to a vendor.

Host: What's the role of proprietary data?

Kevin: I’m a big fan of proprietary data. We have to go into proprietary data, into data that only our users generate with us… and produce insights around the proprietary data.

Host: Is it time to unlearn keyword thinking?

Kevin: I think this is a really important point to stay. We need to unlearn the connection between keywords and content because, otherwise, we won’t be successful in those AI answers.

Host: How does AI change measurement?

Kevin: We have to move from ranking to influence, possibly shifting to citation share as a primary KPI. Think: how often are we cited inside AI answers compared to competitors.

Host: Can small teams keep up?

Kevin: I think so. AI-assisted workflows already let small teams publish 10x faster without sacrificing quality. Execution speed becomes the differentiator.

Host: What’s the endgame for SEOs?

Kevin: Becoming the most credible and comprehensive source, so that AI models pick you as the citation whenever they answer. That’s the new Page One.
