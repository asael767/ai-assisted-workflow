**[04:10] Host (John Horn):** When clients ask, “How fast can we rank?”, what’s your first calibration?  
**Milosz:** I tell them link building is like training for a six‑pack. You don’t buy abs; you buy the habit. For a fresh domain, I cap at five to seven new referring domains a week. Linear growth looks natural; spikes look like manipulation. I’d rather be boring and invisible to spam filters than flashy for 30 days and penalized on day 31.

**[05:02] Host:** How do you decide which sites are worth pitching?  
**Milosz:** DR is the last column I check. First, is the site **indexed and actually receiving organic traffic** in my target language? I want 1k visits/month minimum from Similarweb or Ahrefs and at least three ranking keywords related to my client’s niche. Second, I look at **index ratio**—indexed URLs divided by total URLs. If it’s under 0.3, it’s usually a content farm. Third, I scan the last ten posts: if more than 20% carry sponsored or UGC attributes, I walk away; it means they sell every link.

**[06:40] Host:** Talk us through your anchor plan.  
**Milosz:** I mirror natural language. Roughly 65% brand/URL anchors to build entity trust, 25% partial‑match that include topical terms, and maybe 5–8% exact match for true money pages. Never stack exact matches on the same donor; distribute across unique domains. And always pair an anchor with **internal links** on the target page so the equity moves deeper, not just to the URL I pitched.

**[07:58] Host:** Outreach is noisy in 2025. What breaks through?  
**Milosz:** I start on LinkedIn, not email. Step 1: meaningful comment on their last article—quote a stat and add a corroborating source. Step 2: a 90‑second Loom showing a gap on their page: outdated year, missing source, or weak internal link. Step 3: give them a menu—“Want a fresh quote, a data table, or a co‑branded chart?” Letting them choose kills friction. Only after that do I send the email with the asset. Sequence ends at message four; no third cold email ever. My reply rates doubled when I let them say no with one keystroke.

**[09:45] Host:** You mentioned data tables. Why do they work?  
**Milosz:** Because editors need defensible sources. A small CSV of verified stats plus methodology beats 800 words of fluff. We publish **statistics pages** that we refresh quarterly. They earn passive links, but they also give us barter material: “Use this table; just credit us.” It’s ethical and quick.

**[11:12] Host:** Digital PR vs. classic link building—how do you mix them?  
**Milosz:** PR is fuel; links are the engine. Every press pickup gets an **internal-link rewiring** the same day—new anchor paths from the press page to two evergreen resources. Without that, press links sit on an island. I also swap boilerplate quotes for **data-forward lines**: “57% of fintech SEO wins come from industry PDFs”—that gets reused by journalists and creates second‑order links.

**[12:55] Host:** How do you measure ROI so CFOs care?  
**Milosz:** Assisted conversions. I benchmark each link’s cost against the CPC of its primary keyword. If a $300 link supports conversions equal to $600 of paid clicks over 45 days, it’s green. If nothing moves, it goes to the **kill list**—we don’t reinvest with that publisher. We also monitor **crawl frequency**; good links increase Googlebot visits to money pages within a week.

**[14:20] Host:** AI Overviews are siphoning clicks. Does that change your playbook?  
**Milosz:** It changes reporting. We track **zero‑click impressions**—when Gemini or ChatGPT cites our brand. Links still matter because they’re how LLMs judge authority. We harden the brand SERP: FAQ schema, sitelinks, YouTube shorts, and comparison pages. Links are the tickets that get those assets crawled and trusted quickly.

**[16:03] Host:** Give us a 30‑day sprint you’d run for a B2B SaaS at zero.  
**Milosz:**  
- **Week 1:** Crawl site, map entities, draft anchor plan. Send two Loom “value packets” per day to tier‑A prospects.  
- **Week 2:** Publish one **statistics page** and one **use‑case page**; begin at three new referring domains.  
- **Week 3:** Ship first data-backed guest insight; run an **A/B redirect test**—temporary link to a test page, watch uplift, then swap to money page only if the donor proves clean.  
- **Week 4:** Prune anchors that over‑optimize; add internal links from the new pages; report assisted conversions vs. paid CPC baseline.

**[18:27] Host:** What’s your stance on PBNs in 2025?  
**Milosz:** Too expensive for too little trust. Google is better at pattern detection; LLMs ignore them. I prefer building **micro‑brands**—small topical sites with real traffic. They cost more up front, but they pass **topical PageRank** and survive core updates.

**[20:05] Host:** Any quick wins listeners can implement this week?  
**Milosz:** Build a **“stat block”** on your top three money pages: 3–5 fresh stats with sources and dates. Then, when you pitch, you’re not just asking for a link—you’re offering a pre-made citation. It converts cold outreach into collaboration.

**[21:32] Host:** Final thought?  
**Milosz:** Treat links like a portfolio. Diversify anchors, pace velocity, and sell losers fast. The future isn’t linkless; it’s accountability‑driven.
