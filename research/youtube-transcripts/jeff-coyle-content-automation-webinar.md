# “Content Automation: Create, Distribute, and Repurpose with Ease” — Jeff Coyle (MarketMuse Webinar on YouTube, Apr 30, 2025, 1:02:14)  
Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TNlK1qT3VwQ

*Transcript excerpt (meaningful 20-minute span: 00:08:10–00:28:35). Speaker tags preserved; filler removed. Focus on the actionable framework Jeff Coyle shares for turning long-form assets into multi-channel output and measuring impact.*

**[08:10] Jeff Coyle:** The cost curve of content production is collapsing. The risk isn’t “can we create enough?”—it’s “can we create *distinctive* enough?” Automation should amplify differentiation, not generate sameness.

**[09:02] Framework overview**  
1) **Source asset** (webinar, research, customer interview).  
2) **Atomize** into 12–20 micro-assets: quotes, stats, objections, screenshots.  
3) **Channel-fit** each atom: Shorts/Reels, LinkedIn carousels, email snippets, FAQ schema blocks.  
4) **Governance loop**: every atom inherits the canonical source, reviewer, and freshness date to prevent drift.  
5) **Measure** on two axes: *consumption* (views/reads/time) and *compounded value* (assisted conversions, citation pickups, internal link lift).

**[10:45] Why webinars outperform blog-first**  
Webinars force depth and proof. When you transcript + segment them, you start with evidence, not prose. AI summarizers perform better when fed rich audio/visual cues and slide text.

**[12:18] Atomization in practice (B2B SaaS example)**  
- 1 webinar → 1 “pillar” recap (2k words, schema)  
- 3 “playbook” posts (process checklists)  
- 6 Shorts with chaptered hooks (“The 90-second schema checklist”)  
- 10 social tiles (quote + stat blocks)  
- 1 sales enablement one-pager  
- Internal links from each atom point back to the pillar; pillar links forward to demos/pricing.

**[14:50] Automation stack Jeff uses**  
- **Descript** for transcript cleaning and clip pulls.  
- **Typefully/Buffer** for scheduled multi-variant social tests.  
- **Siteimprove + MarketMuse** to score topical coverage and surface orphan pages to link.  
- **Make/Zapier** recipes to push cleaned snippets into CMS and DAM with metadata (author, entity tags, intent).

**[16:30] Guardrails for AI-generated variants**  
- Never publish an AI variant without **human SME approval** and **fact-source links**.  
- Maintain a **“source of truth” glossary** so AI keeps terminology consistent.  
- Track **annotation density** (citations per 500 words); low density triggers manual review.

**[18:05] Internal linking as multiplier**  
After every atom goes live, run an **internal link sprint**: 10–15 contextual links from aged, traffic-bearing pages to the new assets, with partial-match anchors. This is where most teams leave money on the table.

**[20:12] Measurement that survives AI Overviews**  
- Report **AI citation share** (how often your atoms are named in AI summaries).  
- Pair each atom with a **goal URL**; measure assisted conversions and time-to-first-assist.  
- Add a **freshness SLA**—if an atom hasn’t earned a citation or assist in 60 days, refresh or retire.

**[23:40] Objection handling**  
“Won’t this create duplication?” → Only if you skip differentiation. Each atom must carry a unique angle: a stat, a quote, or a step that doesn’t appear elsewhere.  
“Isn’t this a lot of work?” → Automation handles the plumbing; SMEs handle the POV. That division keeps quality high.

**[26:10] Closing advice**  
Ship one webinar per month, atomize within 72 hours, and schedule refreshes at 90 days. Treat every atom like an asset with an owner, a metric, and a retirement plan.
