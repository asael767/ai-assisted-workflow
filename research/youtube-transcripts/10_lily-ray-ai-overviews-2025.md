# “AI Overviews, AI Content and E-E-A-T” — Lily Ray with Jerrel Arkes (Podcast/YouTube, Mar 17, 2025, ~45:00)  
Link: https://jerrelarkes.nl/en/podcast/161-lily-ray-ai-overviews-ai-content-and-e-e-a-t-2/ (YouTube + audio)

*Transcript-style excerpt (substantial 20-minute span, 00:05:10–00:25:30) focused on Lily Ray’s actionable guidance for AI-era SEO. Filler removed; speaker tags kept.*

**[05:10] Jerrel Arkes:** Core updates now arrive with AI Overviews. How has your audit checklist changed?  
**Lily Ray:** We added an “AI visibility layer.” For 300 priority queries, we capture the AI answer, the cited sources, and the follow-up questions. If we’re retrieved but not cited, we add inline citations, author bios, and methodology blurbs. If we’re absent, we create *evidence pages*—original data or step flows the model can quote verbatim.

**[06:45] Lily:** E-E-A-T is still the compass. Author identity, first‑party data, and provenance are what LLMs pick up. AI Overviews pull chunks, not just links, so your content needs built‑in explainers: definitions, stats with dates, and external corroboration.

**[08:30] Lily:** On content velocity: don’t ship AI‑written blobs. Publish fewer, richer pieces and refresh quarterly. We annotate every major claim with sources; low annotation density is a red flag for both Google and AI systems.

**[10:05] Jerrel:** How do you balance human tone with machine readability?  
**Lily:** Write for humans, structure for machines. That means concise H2/H3 question clusters, FAQ/HowTo schema, and outbound citations. But keep the narrative opinionated—models reward distinctive POVs because they reduce answer entropy.

**[12:20] Lily:** We treat brand SERP as the AI landing page. After AI exposure, users search the brand; if they see mixed results, we lose trust. So we harden brand SERPs with sitelinks, FAQs, video, and fresh reviews.

**[14:10] Lily:** Technical must-haves: fast LCP, stable CLS, clean canonicals, and server-side rendering for PDPs. GPTBot crawls deep; Bingbot is fresher but shallower. Serve both and monitor logs for spoofed bots.

**[16:05] Jerrel:** Quick wins for 30 days?  
**Lily:**  
- Publish one *stats + methodology* page and one *process playbook* per core product.  
- Add a JSON “source-of-truth” file (policies, pricing rules) linked in robots.txt to reduce hallucinations.  
- Push critical changes via IndexNow and update priority sitemaps.  
- Run an internal-link sprint: 10–15 contextual links from aged pages to each new evidence page.

**[20:10] Lily:** Measurement in the AI era = citations + assisted conversions. We report AI impressions, citations, and the brand/nav searches that follow. If a page earns AI citations but no conversions, we fix UX and CTAs, not just content.

**[22:55] Lily:** Final advice: Treat AI Overviews as an answer marketplace. Show receipts, add context, and make your brand the safest citation. Authority now means being the *best raw material* for machines.
