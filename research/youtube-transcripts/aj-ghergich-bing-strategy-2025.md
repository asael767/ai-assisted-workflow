# “Yes, You Do Need a Bing Strategy” — Siege Media Conversation w/ AJ Ghergich (YouTube, Mar 31, 2025, 47:12)  
Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntulrxpXxxY (also on siegemedia.com/conversation/yes-you-do-need-a-bing-strategy-w-botifys-aj-ghergich)

*Transcript excerpt (core 22 minutes, 00:06:00–00:28:00) focused on AJ’s tactical guidance for AI-era search, Bing/IndexNow, and enterprise SEO. Filler removed; speaker tags kept.*

**[06:00] Ross Hudgens:** Everyone says “Google or bust.” Why is Bing suddenly worth caring about?  
**AJ Ghergich:** Bing isn’t just a 3% share anymore; it’s the back-end for Copilot and ChatGPT with live search. If you ignore Bing, you’re opting out of a huge slice of AI-generated answers. We saw enterprise clients get cited in ChatGPT only after Bing indexed their freshest catalog data via IndexNow.

**[07:12] AJ:** Think of Bing as your *API to AI*. It ingests content faster when you push updates. If you’re waiting on passive crawling, your inventory is stale inside Copilot. IndexNow is the fastest way to broadcast change events.

**[09:00] Ross:** Botify’s AI readiness survey said 94% of brands feel “ready.” You called that delusional—why?  
**AJ:** Because 40% of their crucial pages still aren’t indexed. Readiness isn’t a slide deck; it’s crawl budget, canonicals, rendering, and structured data that survive at scale. If your PDPs are JS-rendered without hydration hints, AI systems can’t ground on them.

**[11:18] AJ:** Centralize brand messaging for AI. Create a “source-of-truth” JSON (policies, value props, pricing rules) and host it in an easily crawlable location. Link it from robots.txt and sitemaps. That reduces hallucinated claims about your brand in AI answers.

**[13:45] AJ:** For enterprises, *technical SEO is AI SEO*. Fix duplication before you chase “GEO.” Deduplicate facets, enforce canonical templates, and monitor log files for AI bot patterns versus humans.

**[16:10] AJ:** We segment AI bots in Botify logs. OpenAI’s GPTBot and Bing’s crawlers behave differently—GPTBot crawls deep and wide; Bingbot is fresher but shallower. Serve both, but protect from spoofing with reverse DNS and rate limits.

**[18:30] Ross:** How do you measure success when clicks disappear?  
**AJ:** Track *citation share* and *assisted conversions*. We scrape AI answers weekly for priority queries; if we’re retrieved but not cited, we add evidence (methodology, inline references, author creds). For conversion, we look at sessions where AI surfaces our brand then users arrive by brand/nav terms—assisted value is real even if the first touch was zero-click.

**[21:05] AJ:** Bing-specific quick wins:  
- Push all major updates through **IndexNow**.  
- Use **Bing Content API** for very large catalogs.  
- Ship **faqPage** and **product** schema with rich attributes; Bing surfaces these inside AI cards.  
- Local businesses: verify in **Bing Places**; Copilot pulls local data heavily.

**[24:40] AJ:** “Ghost clicks” are rising—AI summaries drive branded search rather than direct CTR. Make sure your brand SERP is bulletproof: sitelinks, FAQs, reviews, video, and a fast PDP. Treat that as the landing experience for AI-influenced users.

**[26:55] AJ (closing):** Optimize for humans *and* AIs. Evidence beats hype: fast crawlability, structured data, and a clean brand narrative. Bing is your proving ground because its data is powering the AI layer right now.
