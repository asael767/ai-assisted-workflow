It seems like there's a lot of optimism from marketers. First, there was Typeform's survey which found that 95% of marketers are using AI — and 71% are proud of this work or even more so. Then there's Clutch's survey which found that 81% of marketers feel positive about the current state of content, and over two-thirds of them view AI as an opportunity for their content marketing efforts.

What's more, 87% of marketers anticipate their content marketing budget will increase in 2026, with 52% of them planning to boost video investment for AI visibility... even though nobody really knows how video affects AI citations.

As for me? I'm optimistic about some things. I think right now is an opportunity for a sort of reset in content marketing. None of us know what actually works in this new world, so there's lots of opportunity to experiment and explore.

Though I feel less warm and fuzzy about some things: more than 20% of videos shown to new YouTube users are AI slop (per Kapwing research last year). What do you think? Are you optimistic? If so, what about? I'm curious about the opinions behind these numbers.
