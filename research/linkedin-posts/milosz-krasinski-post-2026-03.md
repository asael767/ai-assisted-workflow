# Milosz Krasiński — LinkedIn Post (March 2026)

The SEO industry has been inventing new acronyms for years now. GEO. AEO. LLMO. AIO. Nobody can agree what they mean. Including the people selling them. But the invoice looks the same. Meanwhile the stat nobody wants to talk about: only 1% of users click on sources cited inside AI Overviews. So we're optimising to be cited by a system... that nobody clicks through from.

The dream: "our brand was mentioned by ChatGPT." The reality: to an audience of zero people who left the conversation. I get it.

AI citation visibility is genuinely important for brand awareness and trust signals. We're building it into client strategies ourselves. But let's not pretend we're not all just scrambling to rename the same work every six months so it sounds like we invented something.

SEO. Content marketing. Inbound. Topic clusters. E-E-A-T. GEO. Same advice. Fresh acronym. New conference ticket price.
