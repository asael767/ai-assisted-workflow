# Milosz Krasiński — “Link Building: Why the Future May Be Linkless” (Relevance.com, updated Dec 9, 2025)

**Link:** https://www.relevance.com/future-of-link-building/

The “linkless future” soundbite keeps resurfacing, but Milosz Krasiński argues that links aren’t dying—they’re evolving. In this long-form piece he dissects how brand mentions, semantic signals, and traditional backlinks now coexist, and he shares the playbook he uses at Chilli Fruit to win durable authority without brute-force guest posting.

## Why “linkless” doesn’t mean “linkless”
> *“Brand mentions without hrefs behave like soft citations. They prime entity recognition and topical authority, but you still need earned links to move competitive SERPs.”*

Krasiński explains that unlinked mentions in high‑trust publications seed entity confidence for Google’s knowledge graph and for LLMs, yet ranking lift still correlates strongest with high-quality followed links. The takeaway: pursue mentions, but pair them with a deliberate anchor plan.

## Anchor strategy that mimics natural language
- 60–70% **brand + URL** anchors to signal legitimacy.  
- 20–30% **topical / partial match** anchors mapping to page entities.  
- <10% **exact match**, reserved for the few pages where commercial intent matters.  
- Rotate anchors across multiple referring domains; never stack exact match on the same donor twice.

## Prospecting that filters “real reach”
- Use **server-side rendered** pages only—no JS-only indexation risk.  
- Check **index ratio** (indexed URLs / total URLs) to spot bloated sites.  
- Require **≥1k organic visits/mo** (Ahrefs or Similarweb) *and* topical overlap; DR/DA alone is disqualified.  
- Reject sites with >20% outbound links marked sponsored/ugc in recent posts (signals sell‑side inventory).

## Outreach that survives 2026 inbox filters
1. **Signal first** – comment meaningfully on a prospect’s last article; cite a stat and add a corroborating source.  
2. **Value packet** – a 2–3 sentence Loom reviewing a gap on their page (broken stat, missing source, outdated year).  
3. **Swap menu** – offer: (a) fresh quote from SME, (b) data table update, or (c) co‑branded graphic; let them pick.  
4. **Escalation** – if no reply, switch channel to LinkedIn voice note; never send third cold email.

Templates include CTAs that make declining easy (“Reply 1 to update, 2 to ignore”), which trims spam flags and keeps domain reputation intact.

## Building velocity without footprints
- Cap at **5–7 new linking domains/week** per money site to keep growth linear.  
- Separate **publisher pools** by persona (e.g., PL vs. UK tech) and rotate.  
- Use **A/B redirects** to test donor quality: link from a disposable test page, monitor uplift; only then ship the permanent placement.

## Measuring ROI like a portfolio
- Track **assisted conversions** per backlink via first-touch models and compare to CPC benchmarks.  
- If a link doesn’t move impressions or assisted conversions in **30–45 days**, tag it as “hold” and avoid reinvesting with that publisher.  
- Maintain a **kill list** of vendors whose links cause crawl spikes without ranking lift—often a PBN tell.

## Digital PR that isn’t vanity
- Always pair a press pickup with **internal link rewiring** the same day; otherwise equity leaks.  
- Replace boilerplate press quotes with **data-forward statements** (e.g., “57% of fintech SEO wins come from industry PDFs”).  
- Avoid round‑up bait unless you own a **gated dataset** you can refresh quarterly.

## AI Overviews and LLM visibility
Krasiński shows screenshots where client brands appear in Gemini/ChatGPT answers without clicks. He still tracks these as **“zero-click impressions”**, using them to justify **brand SERP hardening**: own the FAQ schema, sitelinks, YouTube shorts, and two comparison pages. Links remain the lever that gets those assets crawled and trusted.

## Playbook: 30-day sprint (condensed)
Week 1: crawl, map anchors, gather entity gaps; ship two “value packet” Looms/day.  
Week 2: publish one **statistics page** and one **use-case page**; start link velocity at 3 domains.  
Week 3: ship first data-backed guest insight to tier‑A prospect; run A/B redirect test on two mid‑tier domains.  
Week 4: prune underperforming anchors; re‑internal-link to new assets; report assisted conversions vs. paid CPC baseline.

Krasiński’s stance: links aren’t dying; lazy links are. Treat every link like a financial instrument, with velocity, diversification, and audited returns.
