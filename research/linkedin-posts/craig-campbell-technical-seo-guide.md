Whilst Google’s algorithms have gotten significantly better at understanding context within the English language, grouping topical relevancy and identifying spammy backlinks.. The core algorithm and many of the ways Google’s bots read your website are still the same as they were in 2010, as they are today.

Google’s analysis is still mostly within the realm of HTML and XML with things like your CSS files only really affecting your load time – Which is still a ranking factor, just not a major one.

So when it comes down to actually being able to “Master” technical SEO, you don’t need to be a teenage wizkid or senior developer with extensive knowledge in 9 different computing languages to know it all. You just need a few hours, basic programming knowledge, how HTML works and the final, most important key to it all? It’s obvious.. This guide.

## What is Technical SEO?
Whilst SEO as a whole is simply attempting to influence a site’s rankings within the SERPs.. Technical SEO specifically is optimizing the more complicated aspects of your website and it’s surrounding factors, such as optimizing the load time on a server level or fixing broken internal links and redirects.

In this guide, I’m hoping to show you exactly how we carry audit technical SEO analysis, audits and implementation to a masterful level that has allowed us to rank thousands of clients over the past decade.

## Starting With Analysis
Site wide auditing is as it says on the tin, using software to scrape and review the entire site as a whole for issues you can fix between pages.

Page level auditing is where you optimize the specific page you’re trying to rank for such as changing it’s meta title, a H1/H2 or re-optimizing the actual content and load time of the page.

### Site Wide Auditing
Use a scraping/auditing tool (Screaming Frog, Ahrefs Site Audit, SEMRush Site Audit, SiteBulb). Initial fixes:
- Fix 3 404 pages.
- Check 142 NoIndex pages to ensure they’re intentional.
- Check 7 canonicals pointing to redirects.
- Check 25 thinly linked pages (1 or 0 internal links).
- Review 102 301/302s and 13 redirect chains.
- Fix H1/title/meta/alt gaps (e.g., 41 pages with no H1; 289 images missing alt).

### Page Level Auditing
Optimize meta titles, headers, content, load time, and internal links for the specific keyword set.

## Implementing Your Work
- **CMS choice**: WordPress easiest; others require custom work.
- **Search Console setup**: monitor organic traffic, speed, indexation.
- **Meta data**: CRO titles for CTR; SEO titles for keyword targeting.
- **URL structure**: short, topical, avoid needless folders.
- **Internal linking**: pass topical authority; fix orphan pages.
- **Schema**: SiteNavigationElement, Article/Blog/News, Review, FAQ/Q&A/HowTo as applicable.
- **Redirects & 404s**: clean chains, intentional status codes.

## Validating Code
- Validate HTML (malformed href, headers).
- Validate SSL (Why No Padlock).

## Site Speed Optimization
Optimize server, assets, and rendering; site speed is a ranking factor.

## Conclusion
Technical SEO isn’t rocket science but demands disciplined auditing, implementation, and validation. Apply these steps to deliver fast wins and stable rankings.
