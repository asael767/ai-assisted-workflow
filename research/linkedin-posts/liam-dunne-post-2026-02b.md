Getting customers from LLMs like ChatGPT isn't a dark art. The reason why most teams struggle is because they're rawdogging content and Reddit then calling it AEO.

Here's how I've seen teams approach AI search:
1) They prompt ChatGPT “tell me about [company]” in their own workspace. Statistically insignificant and not representative of what their ICP will see.
2) They're using an AI visibility tool to run reports which feels productive but they're doing little with the data.
3) They're working with an SEO agency that has no idea how LLMs work. These agencies continue to only inform their strategy based on Ahrefs keyword research, analysing the SERP and Google Search Console data. In other words, they're doing SEO but labelling it as something else so they can charge more and seem innovative.

If your SEO agency can’t explain how your content gets into ChatGPT's citations, and how they prioritise topics and off-page based on data analysed from LLMs, you don't have an AEO strategy.
