# The Top 10 Experts at Knowing What Google “Really Wants” in 2026 (Lily Ray, lilyray.nyc, Dec 17 2025)

Link: https://lilyray.nyc/the-top-10-experts-at-knowing-what-google-really-wants-in-2026/

Ask any SEO what Google really wants and you’ll get at least three answers, two caveats, and one story that starts with “well, during the last core update…”. Despite Google’s own documentation, the question persists: what does Google actually reward? This long-form piece distills 20+ years of experience into a practical map of the voices and signals that shape modern ranking systems—especially as AI systems summarize, rank, and cite content at scale.

## Why this matters now
Generative search and AI Overviews have turned search into answer synthesis. Authority, evidence, and trust signals—not keyword tricks—decide whose content is ingested and cited. Ray frames success as aligning with Google’s directional intent rather than chasing isolated tactics.

1. **Lily Ray** — Interprets ambiguous quality/trust signals and translates them into action; focuses on directional intent and durable visibility across updates.

8. **Kevin Indig** — Strategy and marketplace dynamics; connects Google behavior to economic/product incentives.  
9. **Crystal Carter** — Structured data and SERP feature expert; makes content machine-readable and eligible.

## Common authority signals Ray highlights
- Durable topical association and repeated contributions in a niche.  
- Evidence-driven analysis over speculation.  
- Clear specialization (entities, news, international, structured data).  
- Consistent educational output and transparent methodology.

## Takeaways for practitioners
- Build entity-first, evidence-rich content that AI can ground and cite.  
- Track which experts (and their patterns) map to your niche; emulate their rigor, not their tactics.  
- Authority compounds: repeated, high-signal contributions across updates outlast short-term hacks.  
- Treat Google’s evolution as intent-driven; align with what it’s trying to reward, not just what changed last week.
