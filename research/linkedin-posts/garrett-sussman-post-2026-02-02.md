The AI Search / SEO Industry is filled with a wide range of great perspectives. Many I agree with. Many, I do not. That's okay. After a couple of weeks with my family, finishing The Black Swan, trying to recreate Orbitwise with Google's Antigravity, and reading a bunch of Gary Marcus, Ed Zitron, and Cory Doctorow, I’ve got some hot takes on AI Search heading into 2026:

AI Search in 2026 is breaking shared reality. Most SEO advice still assumes a single set of rules. That assumption is failing. Blanket SEO statements are obsolete. Personalized AI search means there is no single SERP, no default result, no stable winner. Context, history, intent, and language shape outcomes. Different industries and topics have different rules. Things look predictable until they are not. The turkey never sees Thanksgiving coming. Unknown unknowns matter more than trend charts. Quality is contextual, not universal. You can believe something deserves to rank and still be wrong.

Google does not share our taste or ethics. Results are the results. Forbes printed money with affiliate comparisons until a manual action shut it down for site reputation abuse. One day you are rewarded. The next day you are not. People search through their own lens in their own filter bubble. Most people do not share your taste, skepticism, or AI fatigue. Some prefer simplified content. Some trust-generated answers. Some want speed over depth. Personalization amplifies this gap.

There is no average user anymore. Listicles survive because they work for specific audiences. Self-serving does not equal useless. In many verticals, there are no truly objective alternatives. Remove agency lists, and you get pay-to-play aggregates consumed the same way. AI Search rewards what satisfies intent, not what feels principled. Authority compounds unevenly. AI models inherit bias from citations, mentions, and repetition. Once a brand enters the loop, it stays there. Visibility feeds visibility. It's a virtuous cycle. Fairness is irrelevant to the mechanism.
