# AI Overviews & AEO Playbook: How to Win Zero-Click Visibility (Lily Ray, Amsive/LinkedIn article, March 3, 2026)

Link: https://www.amsive.com/insights/seo/answer-engine-optimization-complete-guide-ai-search-visibility/  
(Shared by Lily Ray on LinkedIn with full article text)

Lily Ray lays out a tactical framework for “answer engine optimization” (AEO) to earn citations and brand impressions inside AI Overviews and generative search. Below is the key content from the article (kept intact and substantial for playbook use).

---

### 1) Start with intent clusters, not keywords  
Map each money topic to a **query fan-out**: informational, evaluative, transactional, post-purchase. Build coverage that answers every branch with distinct assets (FAQs, comparisons, how‑to, pros/cons).

### 2) Build “evidence pages” that models can quote  
- Original data + methodology blurbs on-page.  
- Dated “stat blocks” at the top (3–5 stats with sources).  
- Inline citations and outbound links to authoritative sources.  
- Authorship, reviewer, and last-reviewed dates visible.

### 3) Structure for machine readability  
- FAQ, HowTo, Product, and Article schema; add `speakable` where appropriate.  
- Clean headings (H2/H3 question clusters), short paragraphs, bullet lists.  
- Server-side rendered or ISR; avoid JS-only content for PDPs.  
- “Source-of-truth” JSON linked in robots.txt with policies, pricing, and brand claims to cut hallucinations.

### 4) Speed recrawl and freshness  
- Use **IndexNow** + priority XML sitemaps for deltas.  
- Publish change logs on key templates; update `lastmod`.  
- For newsy or seasonal pages, pre-render and push URLs when inventory or pricing changes.

### 5) Optimize for citation share (the new KPI)  
- Track weekly: retrieved vs. cited vs. ignored for 300 priority queries.  
- If retrieved but not cited: add inline evidence, tighten summaries, strengthen author/reviewer signals.  
- If absent: ship an evidence page and run internal‑link sprints from aged, relevant URLs.

### 6) Link strategy that feeds AI answers  
- Favor contextual links from research-style pages; avoid sponsor-heavy donors.  
- Anchor mix: ~60% brand/URL, 30% partial, <10% exact across unique domains.  
- Internal links: 10–15 contextual links from top traffic pages to every new evidence asset.

### 7) Measure zero-click impact  
- Report AI impressions, citation count, and **assisted conversions** (brand/nav searches post‑exposure).  
- Pair each cited page with a primary goal URL; fix UX/CTAs if citations don’t convert.

### 8) Governance and QA  
- Human SMEs review any AI-assisted copy; maintain glossary for consistency.  
- Monitor log files separately for Bingbot, Googlebot, GPTBot; block spoofed agents via reverse DNS.  
- Accessibility and Core Web Vitals baked into AEO scorecards.

**30‑Day AEO Sprint (Lily’s checklist)**  
Week 1: Crawl + entity map; pick 10 priority query clusters; draft two evidence pages.  
Week 2: Publish evidence pages; add FAQ/HowTo schema; launch IndexNow + refreshed sitemaps.  
Week 3: Secure 5 contextual links; run internal‑link sprint; add stat blocks to top money pages.  
Week 4: Measure citation share; patch pages retrieved/not‑cited; update CTAs for conversion lift.

Bottom line: Treat AI Overviews as an answer marketplace. Your job is to supply the highest‑trust raw material—fast, structured, and evidence‑rich—so models pick and cite you even when users never click.
